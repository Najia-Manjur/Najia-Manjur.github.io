MSc Thesis

(ongoing)

Analyzing the impact of users’ personality and culture on their rating behavior

Supervisor: Dr. Julita Vassileva

Rating scales play a vital role in collecting the post consumption feedback data from the users of a recommender system in order to offer each user a personalized experience. Sometimes users' personality, culture and the emotional connotation of the rating scales can potentially manipulate user's mindset and their behavioral pattern, resulting in a biased feedback. Understanding the influential factors behind such behavior is important to keep the efficiency of the system intact.

We implemented a web-based platform to collect data regarding users’ personality, culture and their ratings on a number of products using different rating scales. The non parametric Wilcoxon Signed Rank test showed a statistically significant cognitive impact of users' personality and culture on their rating behavior. Results showed that extroverts and collectivists provide a biased feedback in a color-coded rating scale compared to a monochromatic scale. Moreover, Apriori algorithm helped to discover a number of interesting patterns regarding how users adjust their rating scores in case of a biased feedback.

Work in progress
Analyzing the factors influencing user contribution and predicting user involvement on Stack Overflow.

Stack Overflow may appear intimidating to new users which may explain why more than half of its users contribute to the community only once. In order to identify the underlying reasons behind such behavior, we collected data from Stack Exchange Data Dump and analyzed the responses received by the new users.

Our analysis showed that, getting no or minimal answers and comments, receiving downvotes and getting demeaning comments from their counterparts can discourage the participation of new users, however upvotes do not play any such significant role. We leveraged these factors and developed a model using SVM to predict the future involvement of the users.

December 2016
Smart-NDA: A cloud based framework for smart device integrated automated neurodevelopmental disorder screening tool
International Conference on Medical Engineering, Health Informatics and Technology (MediTec) 2016

In Bangladesh, the detection of neurodevelopmental disorder gets delayed due to the lack of awareness and resources. Hence, proper initiative is not taken timely which causes permanant distress to the child. We proposed a cloud based, smart device integrated framework to screen such disability, which takes advantage of the questionnaires of RNDA with illustrations. Our tool is expected to increase the rate of early detection of neurodevelopmental disorder in Bangladesh.
